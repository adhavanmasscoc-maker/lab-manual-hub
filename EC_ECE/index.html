EX NO: 1 - Frequency Response of CE Amplifier With & Without Feedback

AIM

To design, construct and test the frequency response of the CE amplifier with & without feedback.

APPARATUS REQUIRED

RPS (0-30V), Function Generator, CRO/DSO, BJT (BC107/BC547), Resistors, Capacitors (1µF), Bread Board.

THEORY & FORMULAS

A CE amplifier with voltage series negative feedback reduces overall voltage gain but significantly extends the bandwidth, providing a flatter mid-band gain and improving linearity.
Gain with feedback: Avf = Av / (1 + Avβ)

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the CE amplifier circuit as per the diagram on the breadboard.
  2. Apply DC biasing (Vcc = 12V) using the RPS.
  3. Provide the input AC signal using the Function Generator.
  4. Observe the output waveforms on the CRO/DSO.
  5. Vary the frequency, note the output amplitude, and tabulate the readings.
  6. Calculate the Gain in dB: Gain = 20 log10(Vo / Vin).
  7. Repeat the process with the feedback loop connected and plot the Frequency Response graph.

RESULT

The frequency response of the CE amplifier (with and without feedback) was successfully constructed and its performance tested.

EX NO: 2 - RC Phase Shift Oscillator

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the RC phase shift Oscillator.

THEORY & FORMULAS

Uses three identical RC networks to provide 180° phase shift. The common-emitter amplifier provides the remaining 180° for sustained positive feedback (total 360°).
Frequency of Oscillation: f = 1 / (2πRC√6)
Gain Condition: |Av| ≥ 29 (Attenuation of three RC sections is 1/29).

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the circuit utilizing a BJT (BC547) and a 3-stage RC network.
  2. Provide the necessary DC biasing to the circuit.
  3. Measure the continuous sine wave output across the collector terminal on the CRO.
  4. Record the amplitude and time period of the oscillation.
  5. Tabulate the readings and plot the output graph.

RESULT

The RC phase shift oscillator was constructed and its performance was tested successfully.

EX NO: 3 - Wien Bridge Oscillator

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the Wien bridge oscillator.

THEORY & FORMULAS

Generates highly stable sine waves using an RC feedback network. The circuit provides zero phase shift at the resonant frequency through a series RC (high-pass) and parallel RC (low-pass) combination.
Frequency of Oscillation: f = 1 / (2πRC) (Assuming R1=R2=R and C1=C2=C)

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the Wien bridge circuit using 2 transistors (BC547).
  2. Ensure correct placement of series and parallel RC bridge arms.
  3. Apply the DC power supply to the circuit.
  4. Observe the sinusoidal output waveform on the CRO/DSO.
  5. Record amplitude and time period, and plot the characteristic graph.

RESULT

The Wien bridge oscillator was successfully constructed and tested.

EX NO: 4 - Hartley Oscillator

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the Hartley oscillator.

THEORY & FORMULAS

Employs positive feedback through an inductive voltage divider (tapped coil or L1 & L2) in an LC tank circuit, paralleled by a capacitor. The tap point feeds back to the amplifier input.
Resonant Frequency: f = 1 / (2π√(LC)) where L = L1 + L2 (for uncoupled coils).

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the circuit using the BJT and LC tank network (Decade Inductance Box and 100pF capacitors).
  2. Apply DC biasing to power the circuit.
  3. Wait for oscillations to stabilize.
  4. Read the output sine wave on the CRO/DSO.
  5. Tabulate the time period and amplitude, and plot the graph.

RESULT

The Hartley oscillator was constructed and its performance verified.

EX NO: 5 - Astable Multivibrator

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the Astable multi-vibrator.

THEORY & FORMULAS

A free-running oscillator generating continuous square waves using two cross-coupled BJT amplifiers. Positive feedback forces one transistor to saturate while the other cuts off, alternating continuously.
Time Period: T ≈ 1.38 RC (for symmetrical components R1=R2=R and C1=C2=C).

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Connect two NPN transistors (BC547) in a cross-coupled configuration with appropriate base/collector resistors and coupling capacitors.
  2. Provide the necessary DC power supply (Vcc).
  3. Connect CRO probes to the base and collector of Q1 and Q2.
  4. Observe the switching square waveforms.
  5. Tabulate the amplitude and time period, and plot the graphs for both base and collector.

RESULT

The Astable multi-vibrator was successfully constructed and tested.

EX NO: 6 - Schmitt Trigger

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the Schmitt Trigger based on BJT.

THEORY & FORMULAS

Converts irregular or noisy waveforms into clean square waves using positive feedback via a common emitter resistor, providing hysteresis for noise-immune switching.
Upper Threshold: V_UT ≈ V_E + V_BE (Voltage where Q1 turns ON).
Lower Threshold: V_LT (Voltage where Q1 turns OFF, lower than V_UT).

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the circuit with two BJTs sharing a common emitter resistor (Re).
  2. Apply DC bias and inject a sine wave input from the Function Generator.
  3. Observe the input sine wave and the resulting output square wave simultaneously on the CRO.
  4. Note the Upper Threshold (V_UT) and Lower Threshold (V_LT) voltage points.
  5. Tabulate the readings and plot the transfer characteristics (hysteresis loop).

RESULT

The Schmitt Trigger was constructed and its hysteresis performance successfully verified.

EX NO: 7 - UJT Relaxation Oscillator

AIM

To design, construct and test the performance of the UJT relaxation Oscillator.

THEORY & FORMULAS

Generates a sawtooth waveform by continuously charging and discharging a capacitor through a UJT. When capacitor voltage reaches the peak voltage (Vp), the UJT turns ON and discharges it to the valley voltage (Vv).
Frequency: f ≈ 1 / [RC * ln(1 / (1 - η))] (where η is the intrinsic standoff ratio).

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP)

  1. Construct the circuit using a UJT (2N2646), a 0.01µF capacitor, and timing resistors.
  2. Apply the DC supply voltage.
  3. Observe the sawtooth wave at the Emitter terminal on the CRO.
  4. Observe the sharp trigger pulses at the Base 1 and Base 2 terminals.
  5. Record amplitudes and time periods for the waveforms.

RESULT

The UJT relaxation oscillator was successfully designed, constructed, and tested.

EX NO: 8 to 12 - LT Spice Simulations

AIM

To construct and test the performance of various electronic circuits using LT Spice simulation software.

  • Exp 8: Astable Multivibrator using LT Spice
  • Exp 9: Schmitt Trigger using LT Spice
  • Exp 10: RC Phase Shift Oscillator using LT Spice
  • Exp 11: Hartley Oscillator using LT Spice
  • Exp 12: Frequency Response of CE Amplifier with and without Feedback using LT Spice

APPARATUS REQUIRED

Personal Computer system, LT Spice software.

PROCEDURE (STEP-BY-STEP FOR ALL SIMULATIONS)

  1. Open the LT Spice software application and create a new schematic.
  2. Draw the respective circuit as per the circuit diagram using the component library (Transistors, Resistors, Capacitors, Inductors, Voltage sources).
  3. Right-click to edit and assign exact values to all components.
  4. Configure the simulation window: Use .tran for transient (time-domain) analysis or .ac for AC sweep (frequency response) analysis.
  5. Run the simulation.
  6. Fix the voltage probe to the required output nodes (Collector, Emitter, etc.) to view the resulting waveforms or bode plots.

RESULT

All above experiments (8 through 12) were successfully drawn, simulated, and their performances were tested using LT Spice software.
