🔁 Repeated Question Patterns

  • Type 1: Geometry Theorems
    In every public exam, either Pythagoras Theorem, Thales Theorem (Basic Proportionality Theorem), or Angle Bisector Theorem is directly asked as "State and prove the theorem".
  • Type 2: Square Root of Polynomials
    Finding the square root using the division method or finding the values of 'a' and 'b' (or m, n) if the given polynomial is a perfect square are frequently asked.
  • Type 3: Matrix Transpose Properties
    Questions verifying $(AB)^T = B^T A^T$ or proving $A^2 - 5A + 7I_2 = 0$ are asked multiple times in Algebra.
  • Type 4: Area of a Quadrilateral
    Giving four vertices (e.g., (-9,-2), (-8,-4), (2,2), (1,-3)) and asking for the area of the quadrilateral is very important in Coordinate Geometry.
  • Type 5: Representing Functions in 4 Ways
    Questions asking to represent a function using (i) an arrow diagram, (ii) a table, (iii) a set of ordered pairs, and (iv) a graph are the most important 5-mark questions in the first chapter.

🔥 Top 10 Most Expected 5-Mark Questions for the Upcoming Exam

1. Question Type: State and Prove the Theorem
  • Chapter: Geometry
  • Reason: Since Pythagoras Theorem was asked in recent exams, there is a very high chance for Thales Theorem (Basic Proportionality Theorem) or Angle Bisector Theorem in the upcoming exam.
2. Question Type: Finding Square Root of a Polynomial (Finding a, b)
  • Chapter: Algebra
  • Reason: Questions providing perfect square polynomials like $ax^4 + bx^3 + 361x^2 + 220x + 100$ or $9x^4 + 12x^3 + 28x^2 + ax + b$ and asking to find a, b are very popular.
3. Question Type: Area of a Quadrilateral
  • Chapter: Coordinate Geometry
  • Reason: A simple and mandatory question that uses the formula to find the area of a quadrilateral with 4 given vertices.
4. Question Type: Matrix Multiplication and Transpose Properties
  • Chapter: Algebra (Matrices)
  • Reason: Questions providing matrices A and B and verifying $(AB)^T = B^T A^T$ or $A(BC) = (AB)C$ appear every year.
5. Question Type: Representing a Function in Four Ways
  • Chapter: Relations and Functions
  • Reason: Questions giving $f(x) = 3x - 1$ or $f(x) = x^2 - 1$ and asking to represent it via an arrow diagram, a table, a set of ordered pairs, and a graph are present in the PTA model and all public papers.
6. Question Type: Heights and Distances (Ships & Lighthouse)
  • Chapter: Trigonometry
  • Reason: The question to find the distance between two ships on either side of a 200m high lighthouse (with angles of depression 30° and 45°) is very important.
7. Question Type: Sum of Special Series
  • Chapter: Numbers and Sequences
  • Reason: The question to find the sum to n terms of the series $5+55+555...$ or $3+33+333...$ has consistently appeared in the PTA model and many public exams.
8. Question Type: Melting and Recasting (Volume)
  • Chapter: Mensuration
  • Reason: Questions equating volumes, like "A metallic sphere of radius 16 cm is melted and recast into small spheres of radius 2 cm", are highly repeated in Mensuration.
9. Question Type: Probability of Rolling Two Dice
  • Chapter: Statistics and Probability
  • Reason: Questions finding the probability that the sum of face values is equal to 4, greater than 10, or less than 13 when two dice are rolled will definitely feature in the easy 5-mark section.
10. Question Type: Verifying Properties of Cartesian Product
  • Chapter: Relations and Functions (Sets)
  • Reason: Questions verifying $A \times (B \cup C) = (A \times B) \cup (A \times C)$ or $(A \cap B) \times C = (A \times C) \cap (B \times C)$ are mostly asked as the first 5-mark question (Question number 29) in exam papers.
